# Voiceover recording guide — Mid-Year Reset cuts

Record yourself reading the scripts below, one file per script. I'll cut
the videos to your narration: scene changes land on your beats, captions
become supporting text instead of the whole message.

## How to record

- **Voice Memos on your iPhone is fine.** Hold the phone ~6 inches away,
  quiet room, soft furnishings help (closet/car works great).
- **Conversational, not announcer.** These were written to be said out
  loud — talk like you're telling a friend, small pauses between beats.
- One script per file. Flubbed a line? Just pause and say it again —
  I'll edit around it. Don't restart the file.
- Name the files `hero.m4a`, `why-it-failed.m4a`, `become-someone.m4a`,
  `zero-days.m4a`, `ai-coach-skeptic.m4a`, `any-day-day-one.m4a` and put
  them in `marketing/promo-video/public/vo/` (or just send them to me in
  chat — either works).
- Record even 2 or 3 of them and I'll cut those first; no need to do all
  six in one sitting.

Target lengths are loose — a few seconds over is fine. Reels/TikTok sweet
spot is 20–40s.

## 1. hero — "It's half-time" (~25s)

> Your New Year's resolution isn't dead. It's half-time.
>
> You didn't fail some once-a-year test back in February. You just
> haven't started again yet.
>
> So I built an app for exactly this. Tell it who you want to become,
> it gives you a few tiny daily actions, and every month your score
> starts clean. Bad June? July opens at day one.
>
> It's July. That's not too late. That's half-time.

## 2. why-it-failed — myth-buster (~30s)

> It's July. Your New Year's resolution failed, and I promise it wasn't
> because you're lazy.
>
> It failed because of how you set it up. "Get in shape" isn't a plan —
> it's a wish. On a Tuesday when you're exhausted, it tells you nothing
> about what to actually do.
>
> So you ran on motivation instead. And motivation is gone by week three.
>
> You didn't need more discipline. You needed a better setup.

## 3. become-someone — identity angle (~30s)

> Stop setting goals. Seriously. Decide who you're becoming instead.
>
> "I want to run a 5K" is a line you cross once. Then it's over.
> "I'm someone who moves every morning" has no finish line. You just
> keep being that person.
>
> And here's the sneaky part: the 5K still happens. It just stops being
> the thing you're white-knuckling toward.
>
> So don't ask what you want to achieve this year. Ask who you want
> to be.

## 4. zero-days — the 2-minute version (~30s)

> This is how you go a whole year without breaking a streak.
>
> Every habit gets a two-minute version for your worst days. Full
> workout when you can. On the days you can't? Six squats, six
> push-ups, back to back. That's it. That counts.
>
> Nobody's pretending six squats is a workout. That's not the point.
> The point is you didn't take a zero. And zeros are the only thing
> that actually kills a streak.

## 5. ai-coach-skeptic — the coach story (~35s)

> I gave an AI coach access to my goals for a month. I went in a
> skeptic.
>
> I figured it'd be a fancy checklist. It wasn't. At the end of the
> month it actually walked through how I'd done. Not to lecture me —
> to adjust.
>
> I had a bad week, and instead of "try harder," it told me to shrink
> the action and keep the ambition.
>
> The thing I'd been missing all those other times wasn't willpower.
> It was having something in my corner when I slipped.

## 6. any-day-day-one — mid-year payoff (~30s)

> Quick reminder: there is nothing special about January 1st.
>
> You didn't fail a test that only comes around once a year. You just
> haven't started again yet.
>
> The app I built wipes your consistency score clean on the first of
> every month. Bad June? July opens at zero. Fresh start, no baggage.
>
> Because that's the whole idea. Any day can be day one.
>
> It's July. That's not too late. That's half-time.
